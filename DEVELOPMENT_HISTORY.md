## Phase 1 — Initial Vision (Early 2026)

The earliest formulation of L.U.M.O.S. was driven by a single question:

> *Why do current photonic computing approaches simply translate
> Von-Neumann architecture into light — rather than asking what
> computation looks like when the substrate itself computes?*

The core insight — a photonic resonant volume as a computational medium —
was present from the beginning. Early documentation focused on the
experiential and conceptual level: what the system would do, how it
would behave, what it would feel like to interact with.

**What was present:**
- 120-facet sapphire polyhedron as resonant volume
- Perovskite nanocrystals as active layer
- Post-binary logic concept
- Magnetic levitation for thermal isolation
- Open Source philosophy

**What was not yet separated:**
- Technical specifications and visionary descriptions were mixed
- Performance claims were not derived from validated physics
- Security concepts were metaphorical rather than physical
- The delay line problem had no solution yet

## Phase 2 — Multi-Model Audit (March 2026)

The Symbiotic Architect Methodology was applied to stress-test the
initial framework against physical reality. Six audit rounds with
specialized AI models identified both strengths and gaps.

### Key developments per round:

**R1 — ChatGPT (4o):**
Established the initial mathematical boundary conditions — signal
thresholds (1–10 nW), Q-Factor parameters, and hardware specifications
grounded in existing photonics literature.

**R2 — DeepSeek (R1) — Adversarial Audit:**
Identified two critical issues:
1. The SNR 0.29 value was initially flagged as a signal quality flaw.
   The audit forced explicit documentation of Stochastic Resonance as
   the intended operating principle — noise is fuel, not noise.
2. Manufacturing tolerances for the 120-facet polyhedron were identified
   as a potential invalidation of the core concept. This led directly
   to the bipolar coating as a solution — an unplanned element that
   emerged from the physics of the problem.

**R3 — Claude (Sonnet 4.6):**
Refactored the Basin Attraction logic into reproducible Python simulation
(`photon_test.py`), establishing statistical confidence intervals for
the 74.2% capture rate at SNR 0.29.

**R4 — Qwen (3.5 Plus):**
Validated material science parameters — sapphire WGM resonator
specifications, perovskite doping density, and Two-Photon Polymerization
feasibility. Confirmed all components exist in research or commercial
form today.

**R5 — Gemini (2.0):**
Synthesized conflicting audit inputs into a coherent architectural
framework. Resolved the tension between performance aspirations and
validated physical parameters.

**R6 — Claude (Sonnet 4.6):**
Documented the physical reasoning in CORE_PHYSICS.md and the
architectural decisions in ARCHITECTURE.md from the Architect's
original notes and audit history.

## Phase 3 — Documentation Restructuring (March 2026)

Following the audit, the documentation was restructured to cleanly
separate:

| Layer | Document | Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Entry point | `README.md` | Overview, validated parameters, audit history |
| Physical reasoning | `CORE_PHYSICS.md` | Why each component, mathematical derivations |
| Design decisions | `ARCHITECTURE.md` | Biomimetic origins, what was rejected and why |
| Process | `METHODOLOGY_SYMBIOSE.md` | Human-AI orchestration |
| History | `DEVELOPMENT_HISTORY.md` | This document |

**What was removed from active documentation:**
- Unvalidated performance claims
- Metaphorical security concepts (Spectral Firewall)
- Mixed technical/experiential descriptions from Phase 1
- Duplicate hardware specification tables

These elements are preserved here as part of the honest development
record — not as errors, but as the natural evolution of a concept
finding its physical grounding.

---

## What Did Not Change

Through all phases, the following remained constant:

- The core architectural principle: geometry as the computational operator
- Post-binary RGB-Octal logic via WDM
- Stochastic Resonance as intentional design (once named and understood)
- Open Source philosophy — no patents, no restrictions
- Vector 2035+ technology horizon
- The conviction that the optimal solution already exists in nature
